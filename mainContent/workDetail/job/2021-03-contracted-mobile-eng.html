Overview

Having researched iOS app development since university, considered whether that habit could be applied professionally, and joined a team developing contracted mobile apps. Learned two things through this team's development: designing feature-rich apps and the importance of work estimation. Learned the necessity of separating responsibilities in feature-rich app design. Understood that communicating work estimates not only clarifies the development schedule but also reveals the cost of app development.

Description

From March to June 2021, joined a team building mobile apps under contract from clients. While the specific product details cannot be disclosed, the work broadly consisted of two activities. The first was new development of a mobile app in the healthcare domain, covering requirements organization, screen design, and implementation. The second was refactoring a lyrics-display shader embedded in an existing app, addressing visual glitches and improving performance. For both activities, specific service names and detailed use cases are intentionally omitted here due to confidentiality.
