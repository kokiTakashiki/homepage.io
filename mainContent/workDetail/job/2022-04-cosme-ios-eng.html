Overview

Description

From April 2022 to December 2022, joined on secondment to the @cosme cosmetics retail app development team. On this team, I took on five assignments: renewal of the product search screen, Xcode 14 upgrade support, renewal of the app's tab bar, API client implementation with KMM, and renewal of the product detail screen.

Of the five assignments, the largest in scale was the SwiftUI adoption kicked off by the tab bar renewal. As the first step for the team and organization to move toward SwiftUI, I proposed positioning the 5-tab tab bar as the initial adoption point, and led everything from building consensus to the technical selection and design. While the implementation itself was shared, I fully took on the rationale behind decisions such as "why SwiftUI" and "how to coexist with the existing assets".

The biggest trade-off was interoperability with the existing UIKit screens. Even if only the tab bar is migrated to SwiftUI, each tab still hosts a large body of UIKit screen assets. I verified an approach in which UIKit screens are embedded under a SwiftUI tab bar via UIHostingController. The verification also dug into non-obvious behaviors, such as push transitions degrading into modal presentations when UIKit screens are placed under a UINavigationController via UIViewControllerRepresentable, and I published the findings as a technical article so that the knowledge could be reused outside of the team.

The renewal itself was completed within a month, while simultaneously achieving a regression-free production release, reduction in UI code and improved readability, and easier tab additions and design changes. There was no adverse impact on crash rate or performance. Beyond a mere screen refresh, the work left both a foundation and team-wide knowledge for subsequent SwiftUI migration across other screens, producing an impact that went well beyond the implementation itself.
